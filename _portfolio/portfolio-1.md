> **Confidentiality note:** All cases published in this portfolio must be fully anonymized. Do not include names, exact dates of care, addresses, record numbers, or any identifying details. 

## Case Title

*A short descriptive title for the case.*

**Example:** Acute respiratory distress in an adult patient seen in pulmonology

## Clinical Context

- **Setting:** Inpatient / outpatient / emergency / ward round
- **Department:** Pulmonology / pediatrics / gynecology / surgery / psychiatry / etc.
- **Patient profile:** Age range, sex, and only broad relevant context
- **Reason for encounter:** Main presenting concern

**Template text:**
A patient in the **[age range]** age group presented to the **[department/service]** for **[main complaint / reason for consultation or admission]** in a **[inpatient/outpatient/emergency]** setting.

---

## Presenting Symptoms and History

Describe the main symptoms and relevant history in a concise and structured way.

**Suggested elements:**
- chief complaint
- symptom onset and duration
- relevant past medical history
- medications or prior treatment
- family or social history if relevant
- associated symptoms

**Template text:**
The patient presented with **[main symptoms]** evolving over **[duration]**. Relevant history included **[past medical history / risk factors / prior treatment]**. Associated symptoms included **[associated symptoms]**.

---

## Clinical Examination

Summarize the most relevant findings from physical examination.

**Suggested elements:**
- general condition
- vital signs if appropriate
- focused examination findings
- relevant negative findings

**Template text:**
On examination, the patient was **[general condition]**. Significant findings included **[key findings]**. Relevant negative findings included **[important absent signs]**.

---

## Initial Diagnostic Hypotheses

List the main working hypotheses in order of likelihood or urgency.

1. **Primary hypothesis:** [most likely diagnosis]
2. **Differential diagnosis:** [alternative diagnosis]
3. **Differential diagnosis:** [alternative diagnosis]

**Comment:**
Briefly explain why each hypothesis was considered.

---

## Investigations

Summarize the main tests, imaging, or other investigations performed.

**Suggested elements:**
- laboratory tests
- imaging
- specialty assessment
- relevant scores or clinical tools

**Template text:**
Investigations performed included **[tests / imaging / assessments]**. The most relevant results were **[key results]**.

---

## Final Diagnosis or Working Diagnosis

State the final diagnosis clearly. If no confirmed diagnosis was available, state the leading working diagnosis.

**Template text:**
The final / working diagnosis was **[diagnosis]**.

---

## Therapeutic Approach and Management

Describe the treatment plan and immediate management.

**Suggested elements:**
- symptomatic treatment
- definitive treatment
- monitoring
- specialist referral
- patient education or counseling

**Template text:**
Management included **[treatment / monitoring / referral / counseling]**.

---

## Clinical Course and Outcome

Summarize the short-term evolution of the case.

**Suggested elements:**
- improvement / deterioration / stabilization
- response to treatment
- discharge / follow-up plan

**Template text:**
The patient’s clinical course was marked by **[evolution]**. Follow-up consisted of **[follow-up plan]**.

---

## Clinical Reasoning

This is the most important section. Briefly explain the reasoning process that linked symptoms, examination findings, hypotheses, investigations, and management.

**Template prompts:**
- What was the first major clinical concern?
- What made the main diagnosis more likely than the alternatives?
- What key finding changed the direction of reasoning?
- What uncertainty remained?
